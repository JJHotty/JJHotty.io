Journal Entry 4


  • Start your timer
  • Understand: Get clear on what is being asked
  • Plan: Plan your approach
  • Divide: Break into small steps
  • Attempt to solve it (write an initial solution)
  • Get unstuck
  • Improve your solution (Refactor)
  • Learn what you learned (Reflect)
  • Practice
    •

These are the process steps handed out in Sprint 1. They are a breakdown of how to learn well within the program; breaking down how to grow in problem-solving.
Be intentional! This is about habit building, Reducing distractions, Creating time and space for breaks. Learning to ask for help, and Making effort to grow and practice. Understand, If a problem arises, you need to know what is being asked, and what are you trying to achieve.

How do you know when you understand a problem? When you can explain it in plain English. If you can’t explain something in simple terms, you don’t understand it. ‘Richard Feynman’
Rubber ducky method is a method also mentioned where you sit in front of an object and try to explain to the object in the most simple terms what you are trying to communicate.

Plan; Don’t just dive into trying to solve it. Give your brain time to analyse the problem and analyse and process the information. Use plain English to lay out the steps you plan to take.

Divide; Don’t try and solve the big problem.. Break it down into smaller pieces. Break it down into sub-problems. Break it down until each piece of the problem is easy to achieve.

Attempt to solve Piece all the small solutions together and attempt to break the code.

Stuck? Take a break. Breath. Are you feeling curious or irritated? Remember the ‘not yet’ mentality. Debug. Reassess. Research Ask

Refactor: Refactoring is rewriting the code- not to attempt the problem differently, but to solve it well.

Reflect: Reflection is important and allows you to learn by trying to understand what you have been going through.

Practice: One sprint will not make you a good problem solver. Solve many problems. Practice. Practice.

This part is copied from the resource.

When asking for help: Describe what you're trying to do. "I'm trying to. . ." Describe what you think the issue is. "I did A and expected B, but C happened." Describe what you've already tried (so you don't get irrelevant answers). "I've tried D, E, and F to fix it, but G happened." If relevant - what language/frameworks are being used, or what operating system and version are you running on? Post a nicely-formatted code block (more info on how to do this can be found in a pinned message in the foundations-help-desk channel), a screenshot of your browser (if relevant) and the error message.
